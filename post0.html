Nutrients and Macronutrients

Nutrient ‘substance that provides nourishment essential for the maintenance of life and for growth’

Posted by Nutritional Balance on Jan 4, 2017

What are macronutrients anyway?

The definition of the word ‘nutrient’ is ‘substance that provides nourishment essential for the maintenance of life and for growth.’ To promote and maintain health, your diet must contain adequate amounts of nutrients. These are called micronutrients and macronutrients.

Micronutrients (vitamins and minerals) are needed in small amounts but are essential to health. Macronutrients (protein, fats and carbohydrates) are needed in large amounts and provide the body with energy.

Protein

Protein is needed for the growth and formation of new tissue, for tissue repair and for regulating many metabolic pathways. It is also needed to make all of the body’s enzymes as well as various hormones. Protein has a role in maintaining optimal fluid balance in tissues, transporting nutrients in and out of cells, carrying oxygen and for regulating blood clotting.

Try to spread your protein intake throughout the day by incorporating it with each main meal. Sources: Chicken, turkey, fish, pork, beef, eggs, tofu.

Fat

Fat is needed for the proper functioning of the nerves and brain, maintaining healthy skin and other tissues, and for transporting fat soluble vitamins A D E and K through the bloodstream to where they are needed for hormone regulation. Sources: Monounsaturated fats - Avocado, olive oil, flaxseed & almond oil, nuts and seed, peanut or almond nut butter. Try to include oily fish 2–3 times a week; salmon, mackerel or trout.

Carbohydrate

Carbohydrates are an important part of our diet as they are used by the body as a source of energy. When eating to sustain energy it is essential to choose lower G.I carbohydrates. It also makes sense to eat the majority of your carbohydrates earlier on in your day and around your workouts, this is when your body needs them most to fuel your workout and aid recovery most effectively.

Hydration

Current recommendations for optimum hydration is roughly 8 glasses of water daily. This is equal to 2–2.5 litres of water. Stay away from fizzy drinks as these are loaded with sugar and additives and will contribute to weight gain.
