Understanding the calorie deficit

When we want to lose weight and shed body fat, most of us eat less and exercise more. Eating More to Lose Weight. Does this work? Well yes, initially it does work with most of us dropping a few pounds or more. Now fast forward a few more weeks and months and your metabolic rate will slow and fat loss will stop leaving you tired and lacking energy.

What many of us don’t realise is that by increasing calorie expenditure through exercise and following a calorie-restricted diet at the same time we are actually creating a calorie deficit that is much larger than necessary. This is why fat loss stalls, our bodies are designed to hold onto calories when food is scarce….it’s a survival mechanism.

It’s all about balance

The idea of eating more to lose weight is a concept most of my clients struggle to understand initially, but it works.

It’s all about balance and ensuring you are eating enough of the right macronutrients to fuel your exercise whilst maintaining a small calorie deficit.
Eating More to Lose Weight

Equally, If your aim is to increase your body weight or add lean mass then you need to feed your body adequately to allow this to happen.
